GearHunter.com

From Concept to Launch, and beyond

The Goal: With this project, we sought to fill a gap in the existing marketplace. We were the first international online retailer for buying and trading new and used outdoor gear with a focus on hunting bows. I was tasked with designing the website from the ground up.

My Roles: Lead Designer, Product Researcher

I conducted extensive research, through customer interviews, reviews, articles, and product videos, in order to determine the best approach for creating a functional website that fulfills the needs of its target demographic. With this information, I designed a sleek and practical website that effectively serves the user. In Axure, I put together wireframes and detailed mockups to communicate my vision to a cross-functional team of designers and developers. My goal was to create a look for the website that clearly conveyed its purpose, to sell outdoor sporting gear, through the design alone. I accomplished this by choosing a color scheme and iconography that evokes an autumnal woodsy atmosphere.

Under my direction, gear hunter benefited from a thorough, detail oriented approach to designing a website that effectively serves all stakeholders.

UTry Golf Club Trial Program

Designing the worlds first at home golf equipment trial service

The Goal: To establish GlobalGolf’s “U Try” program as the world’s first golf equipment rental service.

My Role: Co-Lead Designer

I played a fundamental role in building the product requirements needed to meet the company’s business goals. In order to ensure a successful launch, I evangelized the need for experiential and emotional design in the new program. My goal was to create a product page that featured all available options on a single page while maintaining practical functionality and visual appeal. Designing the UTry system in this way allowed users to easily compare products and find whatever best suited their needs without having to navigate or wait for multiple page loads.

After the launch, I continued to follow UTry’s progress by collecting qualitative user data with Lucky Orange. This way, I was able to assess the system’s efficacy and note any potential obstacles hindering its success. For instance, while analyzing the heatmaps and watching users navigate the page, I noticed that people were not using the drop down menu to explore the available products. In response, we changed the menus so that all options were displayed at the top level.

As a result of my work on this project, we launched the first golf equipment rental service that continues to benefit Global Value Commerce.

Global Golf Deals Banner

Designing for conversion rate optimization

The Goal: Increase conversion rate amongst users that land on the product page of a golf ecommerce store

My Roles: Experiment Design, Developer

This project was done as part of a continuous improvement program for which I regularly conducted AB tests. I followed existing style guidelines to create a new deals banner for GlobalGolf. My goal was to increase conversion rate by more clearly communicating our active sitewide deals to users. I was in charge of designing the banner, writing the code, and analyzing its efficacy through AB testing. This resulted in a 9.34% increase in conversion rate among product page landers.
